Portfolio - Websites

GAARMAGEDDON.COM

I started this site to begin to catalogue relevant, useful or humorous material on the General Anti-Avoidance Rule, here in Canada and abroad. It evolved into a scoreboard of GAAR cases, to help risk management measurements of the liklihood of winning a challenge under the GAAR. It is a home to fiscal art, including various editions of the GAART Board in the Serigraph “Subject to GAAR…it’s Art”. You can also buy many of the works at The GAART Store.

TRANSFERDICE.COM

Transfer Dice is a work of art created following a discussion with a fellow tax director on setting transfer prices wihin a multinational. It evolved into a website that could be used virtually to establish transfer prices and generate contemporaneous documentation. Then I had some actual dice manufactured in China and there are over 800 sets of them rolling around the world, somewhere.

WHENIWASYOURAGE.CA

Remember when you were younger and how your parents could describe their heroic efforts in reaching some apogee in life or overcoming some hardship? I developed this website to bring efficiency and clarity to those teaching moments for my sons, nephews and nieces by being a bit more precise in answering the age old question, just when was I their age and what was I doing?

Portfolio - GAART

The GAART Board, Player's Edition

The GAART Board, Player’s Edition was the second evolution of the Serigraph print, "Subject to GAAR, it's Art"; a genuine working GAART Board and is available in limited quantities. I had this version created to meet demand for true GAAR enthusiasts and to prevent GAART collectors from actually throwing darts at the Corporate or Special Editions. I remind people that one does not throw darts or other objects at works of art.

Les-Huit Coins de la GAAR

The penultimate work in Emjayo’see’s GAART Series, “Les huit coins de la GAAR” is a refreshing, textual, yet purposive and contextual exploration into the GAAR. What at first glance looks like a mere work of franglais, in reality, the work digs deeply to understanding the practical application of the GAAR, in any language.

Tax Inequality

In researching an art acquisition, I was reminded of Robert Indiana’s ubiquitous 1965 work “LOVE”, originally designed for New York’s MOMA Christmas card. So then, I decided to create a GAART work in that style. I also discovered that the art ensemble, General Idea, used it as a base for their AIDS work that became an emblem of the times. The band, Rage Against the Machine, also used it as one of their Album covers, AND get this, that cover could easily spell RGAE, which of course, is french for GAAR [RÈGLE GÉNÉRALE ANTI-ÉVITEMENT].
